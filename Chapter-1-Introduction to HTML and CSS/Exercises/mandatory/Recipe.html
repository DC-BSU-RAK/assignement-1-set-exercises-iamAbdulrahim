White Sauce Pasta

White Sauce Pasta is a creamy, comforting dish made with pasta tossed in a rich béchamel sauce. It’s quick, cheesy, and perfect for lunch or dinner — with optional veggies or protein!

Ingredients

  • 200g pasta (penne or fusilli works great)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (preferably warm)
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (mozzarella or cheddar)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (optional)
  • Mixed veggies (like bell peppers, corn, or broccoli - optional)
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (like parsley or oregano)

Method

  1. Cook pasta in salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk constantly for 1–2 minutes until it forms a smooth paste (roux).
  3. Gradually pour in the warm milk while whisking to avoid lumps. Cook until the sauce thickens.
  4. Stir in the grated cheese and mix until melted and smooth. Season with salt, pepper, and chili flakes.
  5. If using, sauté vegetables in a separate pan and then add to the sauce.
  6. Add the cooked pasta to the sauce and toss well to coat.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs and extra cheese if desired.
